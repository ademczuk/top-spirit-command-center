The pilot in three stages
Stage 1

Prepare

Get your files clean, owned and safe to use, before anything automated touches them.

What you get

Your exports from ERP, bank and spreadsheets gathered into one governed Export Layer, the files you already own, with each field classified and given a named owner. A plain-word Business Glossary so everyone reads the same number the same way. Privacy and GDPR posture settled before any tool runs.

How we measure it

A signed export inventory listing every source file, its owner and its GDPR classification. Status is Ready only when the inventory is complete and the data owner has confirmed it. No automation runs against an unclassified file.

Stage 2

Build

Turn the prepared files into a working pilot your team can actually trust.

What you get

Your order-to-cash flow mapped as a shared picture. A Power BI Desktop pilot that rebuilds one manual report. Document intake and bank reconciliation as draft workflows that never skip review. Every action sits inside an Approval Envelope: an AI draft becomes a decision only once a named person commits it. Plus a ranked shortlist of automation candidates.

How we measure it

One rebuilt report a business user has accepted, and one order-to-cash map the team recognizes. Each automation candidate carries a value, effort and risk score. Demo numbers stay labeled illustrative until client time data validates them.

Stage 3

Operate

Run it, track the value, and decide on the next ERP with evidence in hand.

What you get

An adoption and AI literacy handover so your own people keep the tools running. Before-and-after value tracking against the real baseline. A source-linked Audit Trail where every number points back to its source, owner and status. A ranked Opportunity Catalogue and a Next-ERP Decision Pack covering the brownfield, greenfield and phased routes.

How we measure it

Owner usage tracked, not licence count, with a sustainment sign-off after handover. The Next-ERP Decision Pack delivered as one document with evidence per option. Every claim on the page links to its source artifact, or it is marked open.
