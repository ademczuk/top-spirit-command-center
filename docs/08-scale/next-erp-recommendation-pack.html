Scale - Next-ERP recommendation pack

Vendor-neutral framework. No ERP brand recommendation is made until requirements, data/process evidence and constraints are confirmed.

Status: Draft for discussion. Commercials, timeline and scope to be confirmed with the AI Steering Committee. Audience: Engagement team preview. Prepared for the Top Spirit (Marussia) / Angel Software Solutions discussion. Human-in-control: This pack produces a recommendation and a decision the committee can act on. AI and analysts assemble evidence and score the framework; the AI Steering Committee owns and ratifies the decision. No path is committed automatically.

How to read this pack

The pack is built so the steering committee can act now without prematurely choosing an ERP brand or path:

  1. Decision principles - the non-negotiables that hold whichever path is chosen.
  2. Scored comparison criteria (AC#1) - the weighted criteria, the scoring rubric, and an illustrative filled scorecard with the source each score must come from.
  3. Brownfield vs greenfield trade-offs (AC#2) - when each path wins and the watch-outs.
  4. Steering-committee decision (AC#3) - the motion the committee can approve at the next meeting, the options on the table, and what is being decided now vs. deferred to evidence.

Every number in this pack is a clearly-labelled illustrative placeholder until populated from a named export or document. Real client-sensitive figures are not invented here - see Source ledger.

1. Decision principles

  • Preserve value independent of the ERP decision by building process knowledge, glossary, export lineage and governance first.
  • Compare brownfield and greenfield on business fit, risk, cost, time-to-value and migration burden - scored against evidence, not vendor marketing.
  • Avoid letting today's Power BI / Export Layer pilot become accidental ERP architecture.
  • Keep the decision human-owned: AI assembles and scores evidence; the steering committee ratifies weights, scores and the final motion.
  • Stay vendor-neutral: no brand names appear in shortlisting until a preferred direction (brownfield vs greenfield) is set by the committee.

2. Scored comparison criteria (AC#1)

2.1 Scoring rubric

Each path is scored 1 - 5 per criterion (5 = strongly favourable for that path). Scores are multiplied by an agreed weight (the criteria do not matter equally) to give a weighted total. Weights and scores are ratified by the steering committee - the table below ships with illustrative defaults so the mechanism is concrete and reviewable.

Score Meaning
1 Path is a clear disadvantage on this criterion.
2 Path is at a disadvantage; manageable but real.
3 Neutral / evidence is mixed or not yet available.
4 Path is favourable on this criterion.
5 Path is a clear, decisive advantage.

Default score is 3 (neutral) wherever evidence is not yet available. A score above or below 3 must point to a named source in the Evidence / source column. This keeps the scorecard honest: an un-evidenced criterion cannot tilt the recommendation.

2.3 What turns an illustrative score into a real one

For each criterion the analyst records: the source export/document, the extraction date, who reviewed it, and a one-line rationale - per the docs/05-phase-0/source-linked-audit-trail-standard.md. A criterion stays at 3 (neutral) until that evidence exists. The committee can therefore see, at any moment, how much of the decision is evidenced vs. still assumed.

3. Brownfield vs greenfield trade-offs (AC#2)

Criteria narrative

Criterion Brownfield: upgrade / extend current ERP Greenfield: new ERP / fresh implementation
Time to value Often faster if the current ERP is stable. Slower due to selection, design and implementation.
Business disruption Lower short-term disruption. Higher change-management and cut-over load.
Process-redesign freedom May be constrained by legacy customisation. Higher, but requires redesign discipline.
Data migration effort Lower if schema retained; legacy issues persist. Higher cleansing/mapping, but a one-time chance to align definitions.
Reporting continuity Existing reports may survive. Rebuild needed, but on a cleaner model.
Integration flexibility Bounded by legacy API/export limits. Depends on new platform fit.
Cost profile Lower initial; possible hidden maintenance/debt. Higher upfront; clearer future architecture.
Risk Legacy debt may persist. Implementation/migration risk.

Brownfield wins when

  • The current ERP can support target-state processes with acceptable configuration/API/export capability.
  • Migration risk or budget is not justified now and the platform can carry the business for 3 - 5 years.
  • Data quality and reporting can be improved through the Export Layer first, deferring the bigger decision.

Greenfield wins when

  • The current ERP blocks critical target-state processes or controls.
  • Manual work comes from structural system limits, not just process/documentation gaps.
  • The data-model, integration and reporting needs justify the migration risk/cost, and leadership accepts 12 - 24 months of change.

Key watch-outs (both paths)

  • Brownfield: technical debt and workaround processes persist; future automation may stay blocked by the legacy data model.
  • Greenfield: high execution risk; success depends on disciplined data cleansing, strong change management and clear governance of what is automated vs. human-approved.

4. Steering-committee decision (AC#3)

4.1 The decision on the table

The committee is not asked to pick brownfield or greenfield today. It is asked to approve how that choice will be made, the evidence gate it will be made at, and the work that delivers value in the meantime - all human-owned.

4.2 Recommended motion (ready to minute)

Motion - "Staged, evidence-gated ERP decision":

  1. Adopt the weighted comparison framework in §2 as the agreed basis for the ERP decision, and ratify the criteria weights (accept the illustrative defaults or amend).
  2. Defer the brownfield-vs-greenfield commitment to a Wave 2 decision gate, at which the scorecard is presented populated with real, source-linked evidence.
  3. Approve continuing ERP-agnostic value delivery now on the Export Layer (process maps, glossary, opportunity catalogue, Power BI pilot) so no value waits on the ERP choice.
  4. Name a decision owner and cadence (e.g. steering sponsor + CFO/COO) accountable for the Wave 2 gate.
  5. Confirm that any automation or migration-phase data transformation stays inside an Approval Envelope - financial, pricing and customer-facing outputs remain human-validated.

4.3 Options the committee is choosing between

Option What it means When to choose it Consequence
A. Stage & gate (recommended) Approve framework + weights now; decide path at the Wave 2 evidence gate; keep delivering value on the Export Layer. Evidence on cost, disruption and migration burden is not yet quantified (current state). Value flows now; the path decision is made on evidence, not pressure.
B. Commit brownfield now Direct the next phase at an upgrade/extend of the current ERP. The current platform is demonstrably adequate for 3 - 5 years and migration cost clearly exceeds quantified savings. Faster certainty; risk of locking in legacy constraints the engagement set out to remove.
C. Commit greenfield now Direct the next phase at a vendor-neutral replacement shortlist. The current ERP is a proven strategic bottleneck and the committee accepts the change budget and 12 - 24 months disruption. Cleaner long-term foundation; high execution risk taken before evidence is complete.

Recommendation: Option A. It is the only option that delivers value immediately, keeps the decision human-owned and evidence-based, and is reversible - because all interim work runs off the Export Layer and survives either path.

4.4 What changes the recommendation

  • Towards brownfield: the legacy vendor offers a credible low-disruption upgrade path and estimated migration cost exceeds quantified annual savings by more than ~2× (ratio illustrative - to be set against the value tracker).
  • Towards greenfield: Wave 1 shows more than [X] hours/week (illustrative placeholder - sourced from the manual-report and order-to-cash time samples, docs/06-wave-1/manual-report-consolidation-plan.md) of manual, duplicated work rooted in the current data model, and the committee accepts the change budget.
