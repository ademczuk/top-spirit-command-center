Approval envelope

Human-in-the-loop governance model. AI proposes and prepares, but a named human approver decides whether a financial, pricing or customer-facing output is posted to the system.

What is an Approval Envelope?

An Approval Envelope is a bounded decision space around an AI or automation output. Inside the envelope the system can draft, match, calculate and suggest; outside the envelope nothing posts until a human with the right authority says yes. It lets the programme move fast on low-risk admin while keeping control of the decisions that matter.

The flow

AI proposes
→
Human reviews
→
Approver decides
→
System posts

If the approver rejects, the item stays inside the envelope with an audit trail and a reason code. Nothing posts.

Two examples

Order intake

AI proposes a draft sales order from an emailed purchase order and master-data export.

Human reviews quantity, price list and delivery window in a side-by-side view.

Approver decides whether the order is released to the warehouse.

System posts only after sign-off; mismatches are returned to the envelope for correction.

Bank reconciliation

AI proposes matches between bank receipts and open invoices using value-date and amount logic.

Human reviews high-value or partial matches and exceptions.

Approver decides whether to clear the matched items.

System posts the cleared set; exceptions stay in a source-linked exception list.

Decision table

Bank receipt matching Suggest one-to-one and many-to-one matches Finance controller reviews exceptions > threshold Bank export, invoice extract, match rationale Low-value exact matches may auto-clear; exceptions held
Credit note issuance Draft from dispute notes and return evidence Finance manager approves value and reason code Dispute case file, return reference, original invoice No — always human-approved
Management report figure Compute and flag variance to prior period BICC lead reviews source lineage and signs off Export ID, transformation log, reviewer name No — published only after sign-off

What stays human-validated

  • Financial outputs — anything that changes the general ledger, cash position or recognised revenue.
  • Pricing decisions — discounts, rebates, price-list changes and customer-specific terms.
  • Customer-facing outputs — orders, invoices, delivery notes, credit notes and dispute resolutions.

AI accelerates preparation and surfacing of evidence; the final decision and post always sit with a named person.
