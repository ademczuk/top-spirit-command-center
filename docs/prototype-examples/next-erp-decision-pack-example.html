Next-ERP decision pack

Vendor-neutral recommendation for the ERP migration path. The decision is framed around evidence, not sales cycles, and protects the automation and AI work already under way.

Brownfield vs greenfield comparison

DimensionBrownfield (upgrade / re-implement)Greenfield (new platform)
Up-front cost Lower capital outlay; reuses licences and infrastructure where possible. Higher capital outlay; new licences, implementation partner and data migration.
Time to value Faster for incremental fixes; process change is smaller. Slower; full design, build, test and cutover cycle.
Export Layer fit Existing exports stay stable; automation keeps running during migration. Exports must be rebuilt; risk of breaking AI agents unless mapped early.
AI literacy impact Team learns on familiar data; easier to maintain approval envelopes. Steeper learning curve; agents may need retraining on new data model.
Risk Lower disruption, but may preserve technical debt and limit modern features. Higher disruption, but chance to redesign processes and data model.

Recommendation

Recommended Phased brownfield-first approach

Start with a targeted brownfield re-implementation that fixes the highest-pain finance and order-to-cash gaps while preserving the Export Layer. Run the greenfield evaluation in parallel as a second-track option, with a decision gate once process pain, data quality and total cost of ownership are quantified.

Rationale: It delivers faster time to value, keeps current AI agents and approval envelopes intact, and gives the steering committee real evidence before committing to a full platform change. The greenfield track remains open but de-risked.

Migration roadmap

Phase 0

Evidence & decision framing

Confirm current-state process maps, export inventory, business glossary and pain/value scoring. Produce this decision pack.

Gate 1

Steering committee: choose brownfield-first or greenfield

Need: TCO estimate, risk register, AI-agent migration impact, vendor-neutral shortlist.

Phase 1

Quick wins inside brownfield

Order intake assistant, reconciliation assistant, Power BI pilot. Build AI literacy and approval-envelope discipline.

Gate 2

Re-evaluate platform choice

Need: 90-day pilot metrics, export-layer stability proof, updated TCO after quick wins.

Phase 2

Scale or migrate

Either extend brownfield automation or begin greenfield design with an export-map migration plan.

Evidence checklist for the steering committee

  • Current-state process maps signed off by process owners
  • Export inventory with classification and GDPR flags
  • Business glossary with approved core terms
  • Opportunity catalogue with baseline time samples
  • Pilot results from order intake and reconciliation assistants
  • Power BI Desktop pilot report with source-linked lineage
  • Vendor-neutral shortlist with evaluation criteria
  • TCO model for brownfield and greenfield over 5 years
  • Risk register including AI-agent retraining impact
  • Data-quality assessment for customer, product and finance masters
  • Change-management and AI-literacy delivery plan
  • Recommended decision with go/no-go gates
